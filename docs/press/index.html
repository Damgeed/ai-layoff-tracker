Press Kit

Everything journalists, researchers, and analysts need to cite and understand the AI Layoff Tracker.

Project Overview

The AI Layoff Tracker is a public research database documenting workforce reductions where artificial intelligence or automation was explicitly cited as a contributing factor. Each entry is classified into one of four tiers based on the directness of the AI attribution, assigned a confidence score, and backed by publicly accessible primary sources. The project does not editorialize or advocate — it records, classifies, and cites. The dataset is freely available in JSON, CSV, and RSS formats under a CC0 public domain dedication.

Methodology

Every entry in the AI Layoff Tracker must meet four inclusion criteria: a public source, a documented workforce reduction, explicit AI attribution, and a verifiable date. Entries are classified into four tiers — Direct AI Replacement, AI-Driven Restructuring, AI Reallocation, and Market Disruption — and assigned a confidence score from 0 to 100 based on evidence strength.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How does the AI Layoff Tracker determine that AI was the cause of a layoff?

We do not independently determine causation. An entry is included only when the company itself or a credible third-party source explicitly cites AI, automation, or machine learning as a contributing factor to the workforce reduction. The entry's classification tier reflects how directly AI was cited, not our judgment of whether AI was the true cause. Read more on the methodology page.

2. How do you choose which sources to trust?

We maintain a domain whitelist of trusted sources that includes major news organizations (Reuters, Bloomberg, BBC, CNBC, The Verge, Ars Technica), company primary sources (press releases, official blogs, SEC filings), and government regulatory filings. Submissions referencing sources outside this whitelist undergo additional manual review. Every source is linked in the entry so you can evaluate it yourself.

3. How often is the dataset updated?

Updates occur on a rolling basis. New entries are added as workforce reductions are announced and verified — there is no fixed publication schedule. Significant updates are versioned for citability. The dataset's last_updated timestamp in the JSON metadata always reflects the most recent change.

4. Can I use this data in my research paper or article?

Yes. The dataset is dedicated to the public domain under CC0 1.0 Universal. You may use, modify, and redistribute it for any purpose without permission. We request (but do not require) that you cite the project using the formats provided in our Citation Guide. If you publish research using this data, we would appreciate a link so we can reference your work.

5. What is not included in this database?

We explicitly exclude: general economic layoffs without AI mention, rumors or unverified claims, speculative projections about future AI job losses, normal workforce turnover, and offshoring without AI involvement. The methodology page lists all exclusion criteria in detail. This intentional selectivity means the dataset undercounts total tech layoffs — it is not a layoffs tracker, it is an AI-attributed layoffs tracker.
